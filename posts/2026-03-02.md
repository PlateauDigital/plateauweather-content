Sure turned into quite the nice day! But as is the case in these late winter / early spring days, change isn’t far off…  First up: there’s a total lunar eclipse overnight tonight…bad news is, the clouds are supposed to fill in right before the show starts. The full eclipse begins just after 3am, and ends around an hour later, but the clouds should start filling in by midnight.  As for the weather - those clouds bring the arrival of our next system. Showers throughout the day tomorrow (Tuesday)…some could be heavy at times, and be a bit breezy too, but nothing out of the ordinary. Similar story for the next several days. Temps stay pretty mild too - afternoons generally in the low 50s, with overnight temps dropping into the low- to mid-40s.  An early heads up that Sunday marks the start of Daylight Saving Time again! I saw that for British Columbia, it will be the last time shift as they’re adopting it year-round…that’s worth keeping an eye on!
